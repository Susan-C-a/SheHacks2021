Savings

Saving just 50 cents a day will result in more than $180 in a year. Small amounts saved and invested can easily grow into larger sums. However, a person must start to save.

Why Save?

  • To reach financial goals
  • In case of an emergency
  • Have the option of taking advantage of unforeseen opportunities

Most people tend to use a savings account for their savings however, other options such as money-market deposit account and cartificates of deposit can also be used for saving money. A savings account is a bank account that pays you interest in exchange for depositing money and maintaning a balance in the account.

When choosing a savings account it is important to take into account things like:

  • Interest rate
  • Fees, charges, and penalties
  • Balance requirement
  • Balance calculation method

Interest is a percentage of the money that you have in you savings account that gets added into your account after a certain term. This essentially allows your money to grow as the longer it remains in the account the more money is accumulated.
