Spending vs. Saving

A good practive with money is to portion off how much of it you want readily available to spend and how much you want to set aside in your savings.

The money that you set aside for spending is money that you keep on hand to make purchases in your day to day life. These purchases are relatively small and include things like snacks, a small toy, or an app from the app store.

The money that you set aside for saving is money that you keep locked away either for a rainy day or with the goal of making a large purchase in the future. These purchases can be something like a gaming console, a bike, or a laptop.

BONUS: Try keeping a portion for sharing. Your sharing portion can be used to donate to a cause that aligns with your values.
